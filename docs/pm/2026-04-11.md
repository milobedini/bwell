**These two tools complete the behavioural phase of CBT (sessions 1-3) and unlock a usable product.**

## MoSCoW Prioritisation

### Must Have (MVP — Depression Sessions 1-3)

- **General Goals** — Therapist sets goals with patient in session 1. Patient rates 0-10 at start, middle, and end of therapy. Multiple goals supported. Reflection prompts included. Without this, therapists can't track whether therapy is working toward patient-defined outcomes. *Also needed for PWP programs.*
- **Weekly Goals** — Weekly to-do list where patient sets behavioural goals, ticks them off, and reflects. Ties into Activity Diary (pre-plan activities). Essential for session 2+ homework structure. *Also needed for PWP programs.*

### Should Have (Depression Sessions 4-6 — Cognitive Phase)

- **Thinking Traps** — Psychoeducation on common cognitive distortions. Patient identifies their most frequent traps and reflects on management strategies. Solutions revealed after reflection. Introduces the cognitive toolkit.
- **Thought Monitoring** — Extension of Activity Diary where patient logs unhelpful thoughts, optionally tagging which thinking trap applies. Builds awareness before challenging.
- **Thought Challenging** — Structured worksheet for challenging unhelpful thoughts identified in monitoring. Core CBT technique. Completes the cognitive restructuring loop.

### Could Have (Depression Sessions 6-8 — Advanced)

- **Longitudinal Model** — Extended CBT formulation (Beck 1979). Patient writes one or two, reflects on understanding. Less frequently used than other tools.
- **Belief Change Evidence Table** — Two-column evidence table (supports new belief vs. supports old belief). Multiple core beliefs supported.
- **Modifying Unhelpful Beliefs** — Structured worksheet for belief modification strategies.

### Won't Have (This Cycle)

- **Additional programs** (GAD, Panic, OCD, etc.) — Ship Depression first, prove the model. GAD/Panic only add program-specific questionnaires (GAD-7, PDSS) which are trivial to add once the tool framework is proven.
- **Self-help program recommendations** — Questionnaire-based program suggestions. Nice but not needed for guided CBT launch.
- **PWP-specific differentiation** — The proposal notes PWP uses a subset of CBT tools (behavioural only). Build the full CBT toolset first; PWP is just a filtered view.
- **Admin content authoring** — Modules are currently seeded. Admin CRUD for modules/programs can wait.
- **Programs tab visibility** — Built but hidden. Can be surfaced later for self-help users.

## Recommendation

### 1. Build General Goals (next)

**User value:** Therapists set goals in the very first session. Without this tool, the app can't track whether therapy is achieving what the patient actually wants. It's the anchor for the entire treatment plan.

**Scope:** BE model (Goal with text, ratings at 3 timepoints), FE presenter (list of goals with 0-10 sliders, reflection prompts), therapist review view.

### 2. Build Weekly Goals (immediately after)

**User value:** From session 2 onwards, every session ends with "what will you do this week?" Weekly Goals gives patients a structured to-do list they can tick off, building a sense of achievement. Ties directly into the Activity Diary ("I planned to walk Tuesday at 4pm").

**Scope:** BE model (WeeklyGoal with text, completed boolean, optional diary link), FE presenter (checklist UI with reflection prompts), therapist review view.

### 3. After both: declare sessions 1-3 MVP complete

With General Goals and Weekly Goals shipped, a therapist can run a meaningful 3-session behavioural CBT program. This is the point to do a real user test with the business partners.

## Scope Creep Watch

- **Don't build Thinking Traps yet.** It's tempting because it's the next session, but sessions 1-3 need to be bulletproof first. General Goals and Weekly Goals are simpler tools with higher immediate value.
- **Don't start additional programs.** Depression first. GAD-7 and PDSS are just questionnaires; adding them later is trivial.
- **Don't refactor test infrastructure.** The duplicated mocks are annoying but not user-facing. Clean up after clinical tools are built.
- **Don't surface the Programs tab.** Self-help discovery is a post-MVP concern.
- **Don't build recurring assignment automation.** The recurrence fields exist in the BE model but auto-generation isn't needed for MVP. Therapists assign manually each week.
