## MoSCoW Prioritisation

### Must Have (MVP)

- **5 Areas Model** — The foundational CBT assessment tool. Used in session 1 across all programs. Therapist and patient map Situation → Thoughts → Emotions → Behaviours → Physical symptoms. Without this, the app cannot deliver CBT. Highest priority.
- **General Goals** — Patient sets therapy goals with current/mid/end ratings (0–10). Used from session 1. Therapists track progress against these. Essential for structuring therapy.
- **Weekly Goals** — To-do list tied to the Activity Diary. Patient sets weekly behavioural goals, ticks them off, reviews with therapist. Creates the homework loop that makes guided CBT work.
- **Psychoeducation presenter** — Module type already exists in BE. Patients need to read educational content as part of their program. Without a presenter, assigned psychoeducation modules are dead ends.

### Should Have (Post-MVP, High Priority)

- **Thinking Traps** — Learn and identify cognitive distortions. Mid-therapy tool (sessions 4–6). High clinical value but not needed for early sessions.
- **Thought Monitoring** — Log unhelpful thoughts and tag with thinking traps. Depends on Thinking Traps being built first.
- **Thought Challenging** — Structured evidence for/against worksheet. The core cognitive restructuring tool. Depends on Thought Monitoring.
- **Exercise presenter** — Module type exists in BE but no FE presenter. Lower urgency than psychoeducation (fewer programs assign exercises early).

### Could Have (Future)

- **Longitudinal Model / Extended CBT Cycle** — Beck formulation. Advanced clinical tool, later-stage therapy.
- **Belief Change Evidence Table** — Old belief vs new belief evidence tracking. Late-stage cognitive work.
- **Modifying Unhelpful Beliefs** — Structured worksheet. Builds on Belief Change Evidence Table.
- **Self-help program recommendation** — Suggest programs from questionnaire scores. Valuable for self-help tier but guided tier is the priority.
- **Additional programs** (GAD, Panic, OCD, etc.) — Depression must work completely first before expanding.

### Won't Have (This Cycle)

- **PWP tier differentiation** — Shares tools with CBT tier. Defer until CBT tier is complete.
- **Self-help tier** — Business partners prioritised therapist-guided. Defer.
- **ACT, Sleep, Assertiveness programs** — Secondary programs. Depression first.
- **PTSD, Agoraphobia, Social Anxiety** — Require specialist adaptations. Post-launch.

## Recommendation

### 1. Fix destructive action confirmations (polish — small task)

Add confirmation dialogs to "Remove as client" and "Remove assignment" actions. Three files, straightforward — use `ActionMenu` with destructive variant or an Alert dialog. This is a UX safety issue that should be fixed before shipping any new features.

### 2. Build the 5 Areas Model (next feature)

This is the single highest-value feature to build next. Rationale:
- Used in **every** CBT program, in **session 1**
- Self-contained — no dependencies on other unbuilt tools
- Visually distinctive (interactive cycle diagram) — demonstrates the app's clinical value
- Unlocks the ability to deliver a meaningful first therapy session
- Consistent with last PM brief's recommendation (2026-03-24)

### 3. Build General Goals immediately after

General Goals is the second session-1 tool. Together with the 5 Areas Model, it lets a therapist run a complete first session assignment cycle: assess the patient's situation (5 Areas), set therapy goals (General Goals), and continue diary monitoring (Activity Diary).

### What NOT to build yet

- **Weekly Goals** — Build after General Goals (natural sequence, sessions 2–3)
- **Thinking Traps / Thought tools** — Mid-therapy. Don't jump ahead.
- **Psychoeducation presenter** — Important but lower clinical impact than 5 Areas Model. Can slot in between larger builds as a smaller task.
- **New programs** — Depression must work end-to-end first.

## Scope Creep Watch

- **Don't expand the module type system** before building the clinical tools. The architecture is ready — build the tools.
- **Don't refactor dashboards** — they just shipped and are working well. Move forward.
- **Don't build the self-help recommendation engine** — it's tempting but premature without a complete program to recommend.
- **Don't add new questionnaire types** (OCD, PTSD scales) — Depression program first.
- **Don't optimise backend** unless it's actively blocking FE development.
